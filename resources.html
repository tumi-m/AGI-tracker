Understand what the clock is counting.

AGI — artificial general intelligence — means a system that matches or beats capable humans at most cognitive work. Nobody agrees on the exact line, which is why the clock tracks ten definitions at once. This page is the context: why the date moves markets, and the best free material to form your own view.

Why the date matters — the only lever left

Strip an economy to its drivers and you get three terms. Two of them are already spoken for. Everything priced in a market — equities, rates, credit, crypto — is a bet on the third.

ECONOMY= DEBT  +  PRODUCTIVITY  +  DEMOGRAPHICS

Debt

Lever spent

$324.0T global, still counting

Borrowing pulls tomorrow's growth into today. At roughly three times world GDP, the developed world has already pulled hard. This number ticks up about ten trillion dollars a year.

Productivity

The open variable

~1.5% per year, two decades

Output per hour of work — the only term that compounds without a bill. It has crawled since 2005. AGI is the largest claim ever made about this number, which is why its date is the most important date in finance.

Demographics

Reversing

2015 peak working-age share, adv. economies

Fewer workers, more retirees, fertility below replacement across the OECD and China. For the first time in the industrial era, this term subtracts.

The point is not the exact path — it is that a moved productivity term dwarfs every other input, and repriced assets follow. Forecast dates come from the sources on the clock.
